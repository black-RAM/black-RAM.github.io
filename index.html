The 5 Best Guard dog Breeds

A burglar is no match for these brave, loyal, pups.

From Ancient times, one of a dog‚Äôs primary purposes was protection.   As a matter of fact, before Rome had a police force, dogs protected people from intruders and unwanted animals !

Although many dog breeds instinctually have a protective streak, there are specific breeds known for having the physical characteristics and disposition needed to deter and ward off intruders.

Here are a few:

The Cane Corso

The Cane Corso is the quintessential guard dog weighing somewhere in between 88 pounds (for a female) and 120 pounds for a large male. They have a height of almost 28 inches and a life expectancy of about 9-12 years.
Its temperament can be described as intelligent, loyal, eager to please, versatile, and intensely loyal to their humans. However, they can be assertive and end up owning an unwitting owner.
Also, as with any other big guardian dog, responsible breeding and early socialization with people and other dogs is vital for stable behaviour.

History

The Corso's lineage goes back to Ancient Roman times, and the breed's name roughly translates from Latin as 'bodyguard-dog.' The Cani Corso belongs to a subcategory of working breeds called molossus dogs, named after the Molossi, an ancient Greek giant, big-boned guardian dogs of Mastiff type.

At the height of the Roman Empire, legions who conquered the Greek Islands brought mollosers (molossus dogs) back to Italy and bred them to native Italian breeds.
The offspring of these crosses led to the Neopolitan mastiff and the Cani Corso.

Doberman Pinschers

The Doberman Pinscher has a life expectancy of about 10 to 13 years, a weight of about 71 pounds up to 100 pounds. The Doberman Pinscher is known for being loyal, fearless, obedient, alert, intelligent and confident. Dobermans can be energetic, so they need a home with at least moderate physical activity.

History

They were created in Germany, by Loius Doberman, a hated tax collector, who needed a dog to protect him from attackers. He mixed various dog local protection dog breeds to create it. Making the Doberman an exceptional mix of size and athleticism.

Rottweilers

The Rottweiler is a large dog breed, with a life expectancy of 9-10 years, a weight in-between 80 - 130 pounds, and a height of 22 to 27 inches. The Rottweiler is fearless, good-natured, obedient (with good training) and courageous. Rottweilers are fearless in battle, and its incredible bite force is why many police departments stopped using them.


History

Rottweilers like the Cane Corsos, have a long history. When conquering Roman Legions marched for countless miles and they brought herds as food. The army required tough, durable dogs to move and guard the herd. So the Romans developed the distant ancestor of today‚Äôs Rottweiler.

After Rome fell, the Roman drover dogs found work in the cattle town of Rottweil. Here it earned its name, the Rottweiler by moving protecting and moving cows to markets.

The Rottweilers cattle career ended with the rise of railroad cattle cars in the 19th century. So, they found work as police dogs personal protectors and even guides for the blind!

Finally, in 1901, the Rotweiler‚Äôs breed standard was created.

(Fun fact: Rottweilers were one of the breeds used in the creation of the Dobermann.)

The German Shepard

The German Shepard is so ubiquitous it needs no introduction.   And it‚Äôs the second most popular dog breed for a reason. It is long-lived with a life expectancy of 9-13 years. They have a large variety of weights all the way from an easily manageable 50 pounds for a small female to a man-stopping 150 pounds for very large males. And they also have a height of 22-26 inches which is very comparable to other guard dogs.

The German Sheperd is vigilant, loyal highly intelligent, confident, curious energetic and courageous. And their temperament is due to their history.

Police and military work has been a big part of the development of this breed over the last century, making today‚Äôs working line German Shepards especially equipped with the skillset needed for home protection. Plus, originally German Shepards would herd cattle, making them instinctually always be by your side if trouble struck.

The Bullmastiff

The next breed on this list is the Bullmastiff, with a shorter life expectancy of 7-10 years. A Bullmastiff's weight can range from 100 to 130 pounds and it is known for being loyal, protective, powerful, courageous loving and calm. The Bullmastiff is a moderately sized dog with a dislike for hot weather.

History

The Bullmastiff has the instinct and size to take on a man. It was created in 1860 by gamekeepers in England by crossing the English Mastiff with Bulldogs (which were much more athletic at the time). They are different from conventional guard dogs because they were trained neither to bark excessively or bite. Rather, their job was to track quietly, pin and hold poachers without mauling them. These instincts have remained with them to this day.
